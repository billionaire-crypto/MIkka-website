Q01
IDENTITY · THE SHORT VERSION
Who are you, in one sentence?
→
ANSWER

I'm Mikka Chinbaatar, a pianist and piano teacher based in San Francisco. I'm Mongolian by birth and classically trained, and the piano still surprises me most weeks.

♫
Q02
ORIGIN · ULAANBAATAR
Where did you start?
→
ANSWER

I started in Ulaanbaatar when I was six. My mother signed me up, and I never got around to quitting. I went on to study at the Mongolian State Conservatory, and later finished my Bachelor of Music at MSUAC.

♬
Q03
CROSSING · THE AMERICAN YEARS
How did you end up in the US?
→
ANSWER
HIGHLIGHT

I came over for a Master's in collaborative piano at SIU Carbondale. The American conservatory taught me a different kind of listening. I learned chamber work, accompanying, and how to hold the room without taking it over.

♩
Q04
PRACTICE · PRESENT DAY
What do you do now?
→
ANSWER

Mostly, I teach. I give private piano lessons for all ages and no experience required. I also do collaborative work for singers and instrumentalists, and I perform on the yatga when the program calls for something from home.

♪
Q05
METHOD · ON TEACHING
What's your teaching philosophy?
→
ANSWER

Kind lessons, serious work. A lesson isn't about fixing the student. It's about handing them the ear they'll need long after I'm gone.

♫
Q06
CLOSING · TO THE READER
Anything for the reader?
→
ANSWER

That it's never too late, and you don't have to be good yet. Come curious. We'll figure it out from there.

— WARMLY Thanks for reading. The rest of the site is the portfolio version, with my work, teaching, and recordings. This was just the part where I say hello.
Mikka

— A NOTE TO PARENTS

I teach private piano in San Francisco and Daly City — at your home, on your piano. Children, beginners, anyone curious. I don't hand out a fixed curriculum — every student is different, and the first lesson is where we figure out yours. Play whatever you'd like, and we'll talk about what comes next.

— Mikka
I.
— APPROACH · 05 TENETS
HOW I teach
N° 01 — STUDENT FIRST
EVERY STUDENT IS DIFFERENT
"I tailor my approach to each student's goals, interests, and learning style."
My teaching is centered on the individual. There's no single curriculum that fits everyone — what we work on, how fast we move, and which pieces we choose all depend on who's at the piano.
N° 02 — TECHNIQUE
A HEALTHY FOUNDATION
"Healthy technique from the start makes long-term growth possible."
From the very first lessons, I focus on building the right hand position, posture, and ear. It's the foundation everything else stands on — and it prevents the kind of habits that take years to undo.
N° 03 — JOY
CURIOSITY COMES FIRST
"Especially with young students, lessons should be fun."
Sparking curiosity matters more than drilling. When a child genuinely loves the piano, practice stops being a battle — they go to the instrument on their own, and progress takes care of itself.
N° 04 — THE WHOLE CHILD
MUSIC SHAPES MORE THAN MUSIC
"Listening, rhythm, focus, discipline — skills that carry into reading, math, and confidence."
Music education builds the whole brain, not just musical ability. Students who study music often become more confident learners overall — they pick up new concepts faster and approach challenges with more focus and independence.
N° 05 — THE HARD WEEKS
GENTLE THROUGH THE WALLS
"Every student gets stuck eventually. Those are the weeks that matter most."
Wanting to quit is a normal part of learning any instrument. When it happens, what students need is steady, gentle support — not pressure. Years later, many look back and feel grateful for the encouragement that helped them keep going. Those hard moments are often where the real growth lives.
